# AI Side Business

> Focus: solo AI income streams, productized AI services, and money-proof side-business playbooks.

## Agent Routing

Use this topic when the user asks about making money with AI, AI side hustles, AI services, AI automation clients, productized AI offers, retainers, or practical ways to turn AI workflows into sellable services.

In fresh sessions, start here before [[AI Business]] when the query is about a solo operator, freelancer, agency, local-business offer, client acquisition angle, or proof that a specific AI workflow can produce revenue. Use [[AI Business]] first when the query is about venture-scale startups, SaaS markets, founder strategy, or broad business analysis.

## Scope

This topic captures bookmarks where AI is tied directly to earning money, selling services, or proving a monetization path. It is narrower than [[AI Business]]: the emphasis is on practical solo or small-team offers that can become side income, freelance revenue, retainers, or small-business service lines.

## Capture Signals

For routing, require **AI context plus commercial intent**. Good commercial intent includes direct money-making language, a sellable service, a client/business buyer, or concrete money proof. Avoid routing on raw dollar signs alone.

### Money Proof

Treat dollar amounts and rates as useful only when paired with AI monetization or service-business context.

Good money-proof signals:

- `$500K/year` as an AI service/operator claim.
- `$12K/month retainers` for AI stack implementation.
- `$5-10K setup` paired with a lower-friction AI audit.
- `$999 AI audit` as a productized entry offer.
- `$20k+/mo` when attached to an AI consulting playbook.
- `$200K in automation work` when tied to small-business AI service demand.
- `$8k-15k jobs` when tied to a specific AI-powered client acquisition system.
- `$10,000/month` when tied to a concrete AI content or service channel.

Avoid using raw dollar signs, `/hr`, or `per hour` alone as routing signals. Those pull in salary negotiation, remote jobs, software costs, model pricing, GitHub stars, and other noise.

## Boundaries

Include:

- AI service agencies and solo operators selling implementation.
- Side-income prompt packs, Claude workflows, or agent workflows when explicitly framed as income.
- Small-business AI implementation offers with pricing, retainers, or client acquisition logic.
- Digital products or templates when the bookmark discusses AI-enabled income or product tiers.
- Client app templates, lead-gen automations, and AI prospectors when they are framed as sellable offers or measurable revenue systems.

Exclude:

- Career, resume, salary, job search, or remote-job content unless it is about selling that service to others.
- Generic prompt engineering or coding tips unless tied to an offer, client, product, revenue path, or service model.
- AI tool cost comparisons unless the savings become a sellable service or business model.
- Broad AI startup strategy better handled by [[AI Business]].
- Agency workflows that only describe internal delivery quality and do not discuss an offer, client outcome, revenue path, or buyer demand.
