# Landing Page Image Shortlist

Source concept bank: `ai_automation_consulting_image_ideas.md`

This shortlist refines the broader visual bank for the current SMB AI automation landing page. The page is editorial, restrained, and operational: warm off-white canvas, oversized serif headlines, black CTAs, small muted status pills, quiet bordered cards, and a right-side visual frame that currently works as a square on desktop and a wide crop on mobile.

## Landing Page Fit Rules

Use these constraints when generating or selecting images:

- The image must support the headline, not compete with it.
- Favor off-white, charcoal, muted green, muted yellow, and muted red accents already present in the page.
- Avoid saturated AI blue/purple, neon glows, humanoid robots, sci-fi control rooms, stock SaaS abstractions, and busy meme compositions.
- Keep the central subject readable in a square crop and still coherent in a 16:11 mobile crop.
- Leave calm negative space in at least one quadrant so the image does not feel heavy beside the large serif headline.
- Use practical SMB operating artifacts: inboxes, PDFs, invoices, spreadsheets, CRM cards, field notes, follow-up queues, exception dashboards, routing lanes, and reporting loops.
- If text appears inside the image, keep it sparse and legible: short labels only, not paragraphs.

## Shared Prompt Scaffold

Use this base direction with every prompt:

```text
Premium editorial web illustration for an SMB AI automation consulting landing page. Warm off-white canvas, charcoal linework, restrained muted green, muted yellow, and muted red status accents. Practical small-business operations, not sci-fi. No humanoid robots, no neon, no purple-blue AI glow, no generic stock-art people, no dense meme composition. Clear square composition with important subject centered enough for a 16:11 mobile crop. Quiet negative space, refined detail, high-end landing-page polish.
```

## Top 7 Refined Concepts And Image-Gen Prompts

### 1. Local Business Automation Blueprint

Original bank item: `#10`

Best placement: primary hero image.

Refined concept: A tabletop operating blueprint for an owner-led SMB. The drawing shows a front desk, inbox, PDF/document intake, spreadsheet cleanup, CRM follow-up, accounting, field notes, and reporting as distinct stations connected by clean routing lines. It should feel like a premium planning artifact, not a playful cartoon map.

Why it fits the current page: This is the strongest hero candidate because it directly mirrors the page copy: inboxes, spreadsheets, PDFs, workflow mapping, handoffs, and repeatable systems. The blueprint structure also complements the page's bordered cards and grid logic.

Composition notes:

- Use a slightly angled top-down tabletop view.
- Keep the densest detail in the center-left or center so the image crops well.
- Use thin charcoal lines, small colored status dots, and a few labeled tabs.
- Include a human hand, pencil, or coffee cup only as scale/context, not as the focal point.
- Leave a calm off-white margin in one corner.

Image-gen prompt:

```text
Premium editorial web illustration for an SMB AI automation consulting landing page. A refined tabletop workflow blueprint for a local business, viewed from a slight top-down angle. Show distinct stations for inbox, PDFs, invoices, spreadsheets, CRM follow-up, accounting, field notes, approvals, and reporting, all connected by clean routing lines and small status nodes. The blueprint should feel practical and designed, like an operating system map for a real small business. Warm off-white paper, charcoal linework, muted green/yellow/red status accents, subtle shadows, quiet negative space in one corner. No humanoid robots, no neon, no purple-blue AI glow, no sci-fi, no generic stock art, no dense meme composition. Clear square composition, readable if cropped to a wide mobile image.
```

### 2. Workflow Control Room

Original bank item: `#9`

Best placement: hero alternative or first proof section.

Refined concept: A calm operations desk where messy inputs flow into one clean exception dashboard. The scene should be grounded in an SMB back office, not an enterprise mission-control room.

Why it fits the current page: The current service model emphasizes intake, cleanup, review, routing, reporting, and exception handling. This visual shows that operating model in a solution-oriented way.

Composition notes:

- Use a modest desk with two or three screens, not a futuristic wall of monitors.
- Inputs can appear as small paper/email/document cards entering from the left.
- Output should be a clean queue or dashboard on the main screen.
- Use subdued screen light and avoid blue-glow tech cliches.
- Keep the person optional and small; the system is the hero.

Image-gen prompt:

```text
Premium editorial web illustration for an SMB AI automation consulting landing page. A calm small-business operations desk with two or three modest screens. Emails, invoices, PDFs, field notes, CRM tasks, calendar items, and spreadsheet rows flow in as small paper and UI cards, then resolve into one organized exception dashboard with clean queues. The setting is human-scale and practical, like a real back office after workflow automation, not a sci-fi command center. Warm off-white room, charcoal details, muted green/yellow/red status accents, soft natural light, quiet negative space. No humanoid robots, no neon, no purple-blue AI glow, no futuristic control room, no generic stock-art people. Square composition optimized for a landing-page hero crop.
```

### 3. Signal Desk

Original bank item: `#40`

Best placement: premium hero alternative, leadership section, or conversion section.

Refined concept: A minimal operator desk with only the important signals visible: exceptions, opportunities, and risks. The surrounding noise is abstracted into the background.

Why it fits the current page: The site's visual language is already premium and sparse. This concept is the least cluttered and the easiest to pair with the large serif headline.

Composition notes:

- Make the desk highly minimal, with three visible screen zones.
- Background clutter should be ghosted or out of focus.
- Use only a few labels, such as `Exceptions`, `Follow-up`, and `Risks`.
- Strong negative space is a feature here.
- The mood should be operational calm, not luxury office stock photography.

Image-gen prompt:

```text
Premium minimal editorial illustration for an SMB AI automation consulting landing page. A quiet operator desk with three calm screens showing only useful business signals: exceptions, follow-ups, and risks. In the soft background, scattered inboxes, PDFs, spreadsheets, and reporting noise fade into pale abstract shapes, while the foreground desk stays clean and controlled. Warm off-white canvas, charcoal screen frames, muted green/yellow/red status accents, generous negative space, refined high-end composition. No humanoid robots, no neon, no purple-blue AI glow, no stock SaaS illustration style, no busy dashboards. Square hero image that remains clear in a wide mobile crop.
```

### 4. Before / After Office Split Screen

Original bank item: `#12`

Best placement: conversion section or hero if the visual is kept exceptionally clean.

Refined concept: A small business back office before and after systemization. The left side shows scattered manual work; the right side shows the same team operating through clean queues and an exception dashboard.

Why it fits the current page: The landing page promise is "same operating work, better system." This concept makes that transformation immediately understandable to an SMB buyer.

Composition notes:

- Use a subtle vertical split, not a harsh before/after gimmick.
- Keep the left side messy but not chaotic or ugly.
- Keep the right side calmer, with fewer piles and clearer lanes.
- Include people as competent operators, not overwhelmed caricatures.
- Avoid making the image too dense for the current quiet page.

Image-gen prompt:

```text
Premium editorial web illustration for an SMB AI automation consulting landing page. A refined split-screen view of the same small business back office before and after workflow automation. Left side: sticky notes, duplicate data entry, overflowing inbox, PDF stacks, spreadsheet cleanup, and scattered follow-up reminders. Right side: clean work queues, automated follow-ups, short exception dashboard, organized reporting, and calm competent operators. Subtle split, warm off-white environment, charcoal linework, muted green/yellow/red operational accents. Practical, grounded, not comedic. No humanoid robots, no neon, no purple-blue AI glow, no stock-art style, no overstuffed meme composition. Square composition readable in a mobile crop.
```

### 5. Exception Dashboard

Original bank item: `#18`

Best placement: service explainer, proof section, or inline visual beneath the hero.

Refined concept: Most routine work moves through clean automation lanes; the few uncertain items route to a human review desk. This should read as a simple operating diagram, not a software screenshot.

Why it fits the current page: The existing copy explicitly names review, routing, reporting, and exception handling. This image could reinforce the offer architecture without adding a new metaphor.

Composition notes:

- Use left-to-right lanes: intake, classify, extract, route, notify, report.
- Show a few exception cards branching to a human review point.
- Make it diagrammatic but tactile, like a printed systems board or clean product illustration.
- Use one main accent family with muted status colors.
- Keep labels short and sparse.

Image-gen prompt:

```text
Premium clean explainer illustration for an SMB AI automation consulting landing page. Show routine business work moving through calm automation lanes labeled intake, classify, extract, route, notify, and report. Most cards flow through muted green lanes; a few yellow and red exception cards branch to a small human review desk. The visual should feel like a tactile systems board, not a software screenshot. Warm off-white background, charcoal lines, restrained status colors, generous spacing, clear hierarchy. No humanoid robots, no neon, no purple-blue AI glow, no futuristic UI, no dense dashboard clutter. Square composition, crisp and readable at landing-page size.
```

### 6. Calm After Systemization

Original bank item: `#42`

Best placement: benefits section or final conversion section.

Refined concept: A grounded small office after automation has been implemented: fewer piles, visible clean queues, focused team, and an owner reviewing a short exception list.

Why it fits the current page: The current design sells operational calm and credible execution. This image is outcome-oriented and reassuring, especially lower on the page after the service model has been explained.

Composition notes:

- Avoid overly idyllic or lifestyle imagery.
- The office should feel real: invoices, laptop, desk, whiteboard, printer, job folder, or clipboard.
- Show fewer work objects, not zero work.
- Include a small visible exception list as the central "system" cue.
- Human expressions should be focused and relieved, not celebratory.

Image-gen prompt:

```text
Premium editorial illustration for an SMB AI automation consulting landing page. A grounded small business office after workflow systemization: fewer paper piles, a clean digital queue on a laptop, a short exception list, organized follow-up cards, and a focused owner/operator reviewing the day's work. The team looks calm and competent, not celebratory. Include realistic back-office details like invoices, job folders, a printer, a small whiteboard, and spreadsheet/reporting cues. Warm off-white palette, charcoal detail, muted green/yellow/red status accents, soft natural light, quiet composition. No humanoid robots, no neon, no purple-blue AI glow, no generic stock office style. Square composition suitable for a conversion section.
```

### 7. Systems Map on Glass

Original bank item: `#39`

Best placement: methodology section, subtle hero alternative, or background texture.

Refined concept: A semi-abstract workflow system drawn on frosted glass, with a subtle human silhouette reviewing the map. It should feel premium and strategic but still grounded in operating details.

Why it fits the current page: This aligns with the page's editorial premium tone and can sit comfortably behind or beside text. It is more abstract than the top concepts, so it works best when paired with copy that names the operational problem.

Composition notes:

- Use a frosted or translucent surface, but keep the material restrained.
- Map nodes should imply inboxes, PDFs, spreadsheets, CRM, review, and reporting.
- Avoid generic network blobs; make the flow directional and purposeful.
- The human figure should be understated.
- Leave negative space for possible cropping or overlay.

Image-gen prompt:

```text
Premium editorial web illustration for an SMB AI automation consulting landing page. An elegant business workflow map drawn on frosted glass, with clean nodes, arrows, handoffs, review points, reporting loops, and exception paths. The nodes subtly represent inbox, PDFs, spreadsheets, CRM, accounting, approvals, and reporting. A restrained human silhouette reviews the system from one side. Warm neutral light, off-white and charcoal palette, muted green/yellow/red accents, subtle glass material, generous negative space. No humanoid robots, no neon, no purple-blue AI glow, no random network blobs, no sci-fi interface. Square composition that can also serve as a subtle website background texture.
```
