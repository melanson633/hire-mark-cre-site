# Q&A Intake — Surface Area, Comfort, Bandwidth

Minimal prompt doc to drive a structured conversation with Mark. Goal: move from *demonstrated* capability (what artifacts prove) to *actual* surface area (what Mark can and will do).

## How To Use

- Run as a guided conversation, not a form. Ask in batches; let answers branch.
- One question per turn unless Mark wants to batch.
- Capture answers inline below or in a dated `qa_session_<YYYY-MM-DD>.md` file in this folder.
- After each session: update `capability_patterns.md` (new patterns), `ideas_patterns_matrix.md` (comfort/bandwidth flags, new rows, killed rows).

## Section A — Capability Surface (what else can you do)

The artifacts only show *what got built and shipped*. These questions surface adjacent capability.

1. What workflows have you automated or partly automated that **didn't make it into a case study** — internal scripts, one-off solves, things you didn't think were impressive?
2. What tools are you fluent in that aren't on the resume? (BI tools, scripting languages, cloud, APIs, no-code platforms, design tools, etc.)
3. What's a problem someone brought you in the last year that you solved fast, where they were surprised you could do it?
4. What technical thing have you taught yourself recently *for fun* or out of curiosity, not for work?
5. What's the most complex data model or system you've reverse-engineered? How did you do it?
6. What kinds of UIs have you shipped beyond the labor app? (web, mobile, internal tools, dashboards)
7. What domain knowledge do you have that isn't CRE? (tax, audit, capital markets, legal, ops, etc.)
8. If you had to ship a working prototype in 2 days for a problem outside CRE, what types of problems would you take vs. refuse?

## Section B — Comfort Boundaries (what you'd actually sell)

Capability ≠ willingness. These surface what to filter out of the matrix.

9. Which of the Tier 1 plays (Two-System Bridge, Clipboards-to-Code, Doc Abstract Vertical) feels energizing vs. draining? Why?
10. What kinds of clients drain you? (industries, personalities, deal sizes, decision cycles)
11. What kinds of clients energize you?
12. Are there industries you'd refuse on principle? (e.g., gambling, MLM, lead-gen for predatory verticals)
13. How do you feel about sales calls? Cold outreach? Discovery calls? Proposals?
14. How do you feel about ongoing maintenance/support obligations vs. clean handoffs?
15. Do you want to be visible (LinkedIn, content, speaking) or low-profile (referrals only)?
16. What is the smallest engagement you'd take? The largest you'd want to handle solo?
17. What's the line between "AI side hustle" and "this would interfere with the W-2 search / current role"?

## Section C — Bandwidth & Operating Constraints

18. Realistic weekly hours available for side work — minimum, target, ceiling?
19. When during the day/week are you actually productive on this vs. day-job hours?
20. How does tax season affect availability? (Case 03 implies Jan–Apr is heavy.)
21. What's the income floor that makes the time worth it? Income ceiling that would change W-2 search calculus?
22. How long are you willing to stay in "drafting / building offer" mode before requiring revenue?
23. What support could you bring in (subcontractor, VA, partner, spouse) and at what threshold?
24. What's the kill criteria — when do you walk away from a play that isn't working?

## Section D — Existing Surface (relationships and assets)

25. Who in your network has explicitly asked for your help or hinted at it?
26. Which past employers, JV partners, or vendors might be buyers or referrers?
27. What does the Shortcut.ai relationship currently look like, and what's the upside path you'd actually pursue?
28. Are there local CRE / Greater Boston relationships that are warm leads?
29. Any open invitations (podcasts, speaking, advisory, board) that could become distribution?

## Section E — Risk & Conflict

30. Is there an employment agreement, IP assignment, or non-compete that constrains side work? (RJ Kelly Co. specifically.)
31. What CRE clients/lenders/JV partners would be off-limits for direct outreach?
32. Are there reputational guardrails — things you'd never want associated with your name?

## Section F — Vision Test (1-year forward)

33. One year from now, if this works, what does it look like? (revenue, cadence, type of client, hours, public profile)
34. One year from now, if it doesn't work, what would be the dignified wind-down?
35. If you had to pick *now* without more research — anchor offer, target buyer, first 3 outreach moves — what would you pick?

## Output Targets

After Q&A:

- New patterns appended to `capability_patterns.md` as P8, P9, … with same column shape.
- New rows in `ideas_patterns_matrix.md`; existing rows get `comfort` (energizing/neutral/draining) and `bandwidth` (fits/marginal/no) flags.
- A short `decision_brief.md` in this folder summarizing: anchor offer, kill criteria, 90-day plan.
