## Where data was thin (flag before citing with confidence)

- **Q1 2026 MA permit/start data.** No clean quarterly aggregate published as of April 23. The "4,600 units in 2026" projection is from Jay Doherty / Cabot, Cabot & Forbes (cited in B&T 11/16/24), not a 2026 publication. Worth verifying against any new Boston Indicators / NAHB Q1 release if one drops before you post.
- **Metro lab vacancy figure (28%).** The ~28% figure comes from CBRE via Cambridge Day (10/22/25) reporting year-end 2025 data; Colliers Q2 2025 data showed 29.7% availability. Q1 2026 figures were not published at press time. The page uses "end of 2025" framing to be honest about this.
- **Dana-Farber groundbreaking.** March 16, 2026 is formally site-prep deconstruction, not foundation work. The Dana-Farber / BIDMC press release uses the phrase "construction process begins." I went with "deconstruction" on page 9 and "began site-preparation deconstruction" in the body to be accurate. If you prefer "groundbreaking" for headline punch, the press release supports that phrasing.
- **MGH Ragon JV.** I attribute to Turner / Walsh Brothers — this comes from Walters Group Inc.'s site describing their steel subcontract. The JV is also referenced in multiple industry sources. High confidence but not from an MGB-issued press release.
- **ENR 1Q 2026 Cost Report.** The 12.6% annualized figure specifically applies to first two months of 2026. The piece was published by ENR in early 2026 with March/April updates. If you have ENR access, a direct look at the Q1 report would be worth doing before posting.

1. **The Arrowpoint quote on page 5.** The paraphrase in the pull quote is drawn from B&T's own paraphrase of Lamattina's position. B&T's article (4/19/26) sits just outside the Q1 window but is the most direct articulation of the cross-border capital flow I could find; I treated it as permissible under our "published through April 23 about Q1 events" rule.
2. **The Bloomberg "harsh real-estate reality" nuance on page 7.** I flagged this during planning. It complicates the ERC-as-proof-point story but sharpens the actual thesis (anchored wins, speculative loses). If you'd rather present a cleaner narrative without the Bloomberg counterpoint, I can redraft the page 7 pull quote to remove it.
3. **Labor lean.** Per your direction, open-shop lean with scope limited. The document doesn't mention union/open-shop dynamics by name. If a reader asks, the honest answer is "this issue doesn't take a side"; if you want a one-line acknowledgment somewhere, the "why it matters" box on page 4 is the lowest-risk location.
4. **"Collapse" language.** Pages 4 and 6 use strong words — "collapse," "punished," "broken" (in the close rebuttal). The prior CRE document's tone permitted this but the construction audience is more operational and may read this as alarmist. If you want to soften, the three specific edits are: p.4 headline "collapse" → "slowdown" or "pullback"; p.7 "punished" → "marked down"; p.13 "broken" — keep, since it's rebutted in the same phrase.
5. **Cover dek.** "The quarter when tariff math caught up, the lab boom ended, and New Hampshire started winning." Punchy but editorializes slightly. Safer alternative: "The quarter that reset construction pricing, the lab market, and the regional pipeline." Your call.
