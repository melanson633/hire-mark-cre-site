# Site Strategy

## Positioning

hire-mark.net should present Mark Melanson as a differentiated operating layer for CRE accounting and finance.

Core positioning:
**A differentiated operating layer for CRE accounting and finance.**

What the site should signal:

- sharper systems
- cleaner models
- tighter workflows
- reporting logic
- operator-grade research
- reusable AI leverage

Desired brand feel:

- future-facing
- down to earth
- slightly playful
- elite technical quality
- a little enigmatic
- intelligent, dry, and restrained

Primary audience cluster:

- CRE accounting and finance leaders
- recruiters and hiring managers
- consulting and advisory prospects
- technically curious finance operators
- readers who may subscribe for updates, research, or future product access

Mark Melanson sits at a useful intersection:

- CRE finance operator
- Excel and reporting systems builder
- Practical AI and workflow tool builder

The site should express that overlap clearly instead of reading like a generic personal resume page.

## Homepage Job

The homepage should do four things well:

1. Explain what Mark builds
2. Show enough proof to feel credible
3. Capture interest for future products and research
4. Make it easy to start a conversation

The homepage should route multiple audiences without collapsing them into one generic CTA.

## Product Lanes

- Excel templates and reporting systems
- Small workflow tools and utilities
- CRE research and newsletter products

## Anti-Goals

- Do not let the site become a resume dump.
- Do not make the first version bloated.
- Do not overpromise features that do not exist yet.
- Do not mix raw private career notes with public-facing copy.
- Do not read like a generic SaaS landing page, consultant brochure, or loud fintech pitch.
