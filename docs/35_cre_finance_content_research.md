## 2. Format Matrix — What Works Where

### Website

| Format | Role | Conversion Power |
|---|---|---|
| Deal case studies with real numbers | Flagship content. Setup → numbers → complexity → resolution → lesson. | High credibility, attracts exact target audience |
| Market data + opinionated commentary | Interpretation > raw data. Professionals drowning in data, starving for perspective. | Builds habitual readership |
| How-to guides / technical deep dives | Waterfall modeling, debt structuring, cost seg, GAAP vs. tax basis. More specific = better. | SEO anchors, drive organic traffic for years |
| Downloadable templates/tools | Excel models, checklists, frameworks. Gated behind email = 15-30% conversion rate. | #1 lead magnet in CRE |
| Benchmarking / comparison content | OpEx benchmarks, cap rate surveys, debt pricing. "X vs. Y" formats get high CTR. | Calibration tool for professionals |

### LinkedIn

| Format | Engagement Level | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| Carousel / document posts | Highest (2-3x vs. text) | Deal breakdowns, step-by-step analysis, market data |
| Text-only thought leadership | High (easiest to produce) | Contrarian takes, deal postmortems, personal narrative + insight |
| Polls | High reach, shallow depth | Market sentiment, asset class debates, career questions |
| Infographics / single image | Moderate-high | One-insight data visualizations, deal structure diagrams |
| Short video (< 90s, captioned) | Growing but niche | Market reaction takes, screen-share walkthroughs |
| Long-form articles | Lowest organic reach | Avoid; post key insights as native posts, link article in comments |

## 3. Conversion Drivers — What Turns Visitors Into Subscribers

Ranked by effectiveness:

1. **Gated Excel models/templates** — 15-30% conversion in niche B2B. Professionals trade email for tool that saves 4+ hours.
2. **Newsletter with proprietary analysis** — not link roundups. Proprietary data or experienced interpretation that saves time.
3. **Recurring market reports** — monthly/quarterly cadence for specific niche builds habitual readership.
4. **Deal postmortems / "lessons learned"** — vulnerability + real-world learning = authenticity. Rare in CRE, so it stands out.
5. **Interactive calculators** — DSCR, cash-on-cash, loan sizing. Drive repeat visits and organic sharing.

**What does NOT convert:** generic restated news, vague self-promotional thought leadership, broad "CRE market" content without niche specificity, academic PDF whitepapers.

## 5. LinkedIn Playbook (Compact)

**Cadence:** 3-5 posts/week. Tue-Thu, 7-8 AM local. Consistency > volume.

**Content mix (4-1-1):** 4 value posts (market, technical, data) → 1 personal/career post → 1 engagement post (poll, hot take).

**Hook formula:** First 2-3 lines must compel "see more." Use specific numbers, contrarian claims, or curiosity gaps. Example: "I underwrote a $45M office deal last week. Here's why I passed."

**Engagement tactics:**
- Reply to all comments within 2 hours (algorithm weights early velocity)
- Comment meaningfully on 5-10 posts from others daily before/after your own post
- Tag sparingly, only when genuinely relevant

**Hashtags:** 3-5 niche tags at end of post: #CRE #CREfinance #CMBS #commercialrealestate #multifamily

**Profile:** Headline = what you do + who you help (not job title). Featured section = best content or lead magnet.

**Content repurposing flywheel:** Site case studies → carousels. Research themes → text posts. Newsletter content → text posts. Templates → teaser carousels with CTA.

## 6. Cross-Cutting "Viral" Themes

These themes cut across multiple topic categories and have outsized engagement potential:

- **"Lazy Man's 1031"** — Using 100% bonus depreciation as 1031 exchange alternative. Challenges conventional wisdom.
- **End of "extend and pretend"** — Forced resolution of deferred CRE problems (maturities, vacancy, impairment). Overarching market narrative.
- **Insurance as underwriting variable** — Quiet repricing of CRE based on climate/insurance risk. Under-covered.
- **Continuation vehicles** — GP-led secondaries booming as alternative to forced dispositions. Complex waterfall/accounting implications.
- **Talent crisis as automation driver** — 340K fewer accountants → content about doing more with fewer people resonates deeply.
- **AI trust gap** — 92% trying, 5% succeeding. Investment committees distrust AI-generated analysis. Practical "what works" content fills the gap.

## 8. High-Performing CRE Content Site Patterns

What the best sites (A.CRE, Green Street, Spencer Burton) do:

- **Niche specificity** — pick a lane and own it
- **Show the work** — actual analysis, actual models, actual deal math
- **Consistent cadence** — predictable publishing, even if infrequent
- **Professional but personal** — first person, opinions, smart-colleague tone
- **CTAs that match content** — "Download the model I used" not "Contact Us"
- **Fast, clean, mobile-friendly** — CRE pros often on mobile between meetings

What low-performing sites do: brochureware, stale blogs, phone-number gates, AI-generated filler, no niche, stock glass-building photos.
